# Code of Conduct

## Our standards

This is a small, technically-focused project. We expect contributors
to:

- **Be respectful** in issues, PRs, and discussions. No personal
  attacks, no harassment, no discrimination.
- **Assume good intent** when reviewing or being reviewed. Most
  disagreements are misunderstandings, not malice.
- **Focus on the work** — critique code, not people. "This approach
  has a race condition" lands differently from "you wrote a buggy
  function".
- **Accept feedback graciously.** Maintainer review may push back on
  scope or design. That is the maintainer's job, not personal.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form.
- Sexually explicit or violent language/imagery in project spaces.
- Doxxing — publishing private information without consent.
- Sustained disruption of constructive discussion.

## Scope

Applies in all project-related spaces: GitHub issues, PRs,
discussions, Security Advisories, and any chat/forum/email used to
discuss the project.

## Enforcement

The maintainer (Igor Pugachev) handles enforcement. Report violations
privately to `ipugachev84@gmail.com` with subject `[dev-workflow CoC]`.

Responses, in order of escalation:

1. Private clarification or warning.
2. Public warning on the relevant issue/PR.
3. Temporary ban from the project (1 week / 1 month).
4. Permanent ban.

The maintainer's decision is final for this project. Disagreements can
be raised privately, but enforcement actions stand while under review.

## Acknowledgment

Loosely based on the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/)
spirit, simplified to fit a single-maintainer project.
